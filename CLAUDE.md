14 CFR 91.303 Aerobatic flight.

No person may operate an aircraft in aerobatic flight—
(a) Over any congested area of a city, town, or settlement;
(b) Over an open air assembly of persons;
(c) Within the lateral boundaries of the surface areas of Class B, Class C, Class D, or Class E airspace designated for an airport;
(d) Within 4 nautical miles of the center line of any Federal airway

- "Congested area" is not defined by the FAA, but many people use yellow-tinted areas on sectional charts as a guide.
  - I am not sure what population density or other metric the FAA uses for their yellow tinting.
  - You may need to find a source of population density data, or a geo shape of FAA yellow tinted areas.
- Airspace lateral bound shapes can be found in the FAA NASR data distribution.
  - Remember that class-E bounds are excluded ONLY if designated for an airport (not an extension area or other area).
- Federal airway definitions are also found in the FAA NASR data distribution.
  - You will need both the airway definitions (list of fixes) and the coordinates of those fixes.
